PROJECT

A wind turbine turns wind energy into electricity using the
aerodynamic force from the rotor blades.When wind flows
across the blade, the air pressure on one side of the blade
decreases. The difference in air pressure across the two
sides of the blade creates both lift and drag. The force
of the lift is stronger than the drag and this causes the rotor
to spin. The rotor connects to the generator, either directly
(if itâ€™s a direct drive turbine) or through a shaft and a series
of gears (a gearbox) that speed up the rotation and allow for
a physically smaller generator. This translation of aerodynamic
force to rotation of a generator creates electricity.
